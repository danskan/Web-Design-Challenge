This scatterplot shows the relationship of a city's lattitude to it's maximum recorded temperature on a given date of April 1, 2022. The highest recorded temperatures are tightly correlated to a city being located within +/- 30 degrees of the equator, and then temperatures fall off as cities get closer to the poles of the planet. An interesting observation of the underlying 1,000 plus city data set is that there appear to be far more cities north of the equator than there are cities south of the equator, shaping the scatterplot into an inverted swoosh.
